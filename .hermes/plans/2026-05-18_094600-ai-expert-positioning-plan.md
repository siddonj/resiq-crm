## Phase 3: LinkedIn Strategy (Ongoing)

### 3.1 Post rhythm
- **2–3 posts/week** minimum
- Tuesday/Thursday mornings are optimal for your audience
- Saturday: lighter / industry observation post

### 3.2 Post types (rotate)

**Type A — Micro-insight (60%)**
2–4 sentences. A specific observation or pushback. Never generic.
> *Example: "Every AI leasing demo shows perfect conversations. Here's what happens at property 147 when the resident asks about parking rates during a tour booking. The answer matters more than the smile."*

**Type B — Article drop (20%)**
When you publish on TechScribe, post a short version on LinkedIn with the link.
> *Example: "I just published a breakdown of where AI actually delivers in multifamily NOCs — and where it's still just noise. Link in comments."*

**Type C — Industry commentary (20%)**
React to news, vendor announcements, or industry reports.
> *Example: "Vendor X just announced their AI leasing agent. Interesting timing — I tested it at 50 properties last quarter. Short version: good at scheduling, bad at exceptions."*

### 3.3 Engagement strategy
- Reply to 3–5 relevant posts in multifamily/tech circles per week
- Never generic praise. Add a specific observation or pushback.
- Target: comments on posts from NMHC, Multifamily Executive, MFE, and vendor announcements

### 3.4 Profile optimization
- **Headline:** "VP of IT Architecture @ MAA | Founder @ ResiQ | Deploying AI at scale across 300+ multifamily properties"
- **About (opening line):** "I build and operate AI systems across 300+ apartment properties. I write about what actually works — and what vendors won't tell you."
- **Featured:** Pin 2 best AI articles + 1 LinkedIn post that got traction
- **Activity tab:** Ensure AI-related engagement is visible (comments, reposts)

## Risks & Watch-Outs

- **Hype trap:** If any post reads like a vendor blog, it undermines the entire positioning. Nothing generic.
- **Overpublishing:** 1 good post every 10 days is better than 3 mediocre ones. Quality is the differentiator.
- **Neglecting non-AI content:** Don't stop writing about Wi-Fi, access control, etc. AI expertise is additive to your existing authority, not a replacement.
- **Metrics vanity:** Comments and impressions don't matter if they're from bots or vendors. Watch for real operator engagement.
